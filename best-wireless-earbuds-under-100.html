Do You Really Need to Spend Over $100 on Earbuds?

Absolutely not. In 2026 the gap between budget and premium wireless earbuds has never been smaller. Under $100 you can get active noise cancellation, 30 hours of battery life, multipoint Bluetooth, and genuinely excellent sound quality. The days of cheap earbuds sounding cheap are largely over — if you know which ones to buy.

We tested five of the most popular wireless earbuds under $100 across two weeks of daily use — commuting, working from home, gym sessions, and video calls. Here is what we found — and which one we actually recommend depending on your priorities.

"Under $100 used to mean compromising on everything. In 2026 it means choosing which premium features matter most to you."

1. Samsung Galaxy Buds3 — Best Overall Under $100

The Samsung Galaxy Buds3 are our top pick at this price point. The sound quality is genuinely impressive — clear mids, punchy bass, and detailed highs that rival earbuds costing twice as much. Active noise cancellation performs well in most environments and the fit is comfortable enough for all day wear. Battery life sits at 6 hours per charge with 18 more in the case — solid but not class leading.

Samsung users get the most out of these through seamless Galaxy integration but they work well with any Android device. iPhone users will find the experience slightly more limited. Available on Amazon — check current price as they frequently go on sale significantly below $100.

2. Jabra Elite 4 — Best for Calls and Work

If you spend a lot of time on video calls the Jabra Elite 4 is the standout choice under $100. The microphone quality is noticeably better than most competitors at this price — voices come through clearly and background noise is well suppressed. For remote workers and side hustlers on frequent calls this makes a real practical difference.

Sound quality for music is good rather than exceptional but the call performance more than compensates. Battery life is excellent at 7 hours per charge. The fit is secure and comfortable making them suitable for gym use as well as office work. One of the best value options available on Amazon right now.

What to Look For Under $100
  • Active noise cancellation — now standard at this price
  • Multipoint Bluetooth — connect two devices simultaneously
  • At least 6 hours battery per charge plus case
  • IPX4 water resistance minimum — sweat and rain protection
  • Good microphone if you are on calls regularly
What You Still Compromise On
  • ANC not as powerful as $200+ earbuds
  • Sound stage narrower than premium options
  • Build quality and materials feel cheaper
  • App features more limited than flagship models

Which Earbuds Should You Buy?

For most people the Samsung Galaxy Buds3 are the best all-round choice under $100 — great sound, solid ANC, comfortable fit, and a competitive price that regularly drops further on Amazon. If you are primarily on video calls all day go for the Jabra Elite 4 — the microphone quality difference is noticeable and worth the slight audio trade-off.

On a tighter budget — the EarFun Air Pro 3 regularly sells for under $60 on Amazon and punches significantly above its weight class. It is the best value option in this roundup and a genuinely impressive pair of earbuds for the price. Check current prices on Amazon as all of these go on sale regularly.
