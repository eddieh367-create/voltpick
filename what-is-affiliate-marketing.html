What Exactly Is Affiliate Marketing?

Affiliate marketing is one of the simplest ways to make money online — and one of the most misunderstood. At its core it works like this: you recommend a product, someone buys it through your link, and you earn a commission. No inventory. No customer service. No shipping. Just a link and a commission.

Companies like Amazon, Digistore24, and thousands of other brands pay ordinary people to send them customers. You are the bridge between the buyer and the seller. Every time that bridge gets crossed — you get paid.

"The best part about affiliate marketing is you earn while you sleep. Write an article once — earn from it for years."

How Does It Actually Work?

Here's the process from start to finish. First you join an affiliate program — Amazon Associates and Digistore24 are both free to join. They give you a unique tracking link for any product you want to promote. When someone clicks your link and makes a purchase, the sale gets tracked back to you and you earn a percentage of the sale.

The percentages vary widely. Amazon pays 1–8% on physical products. Digistore24 pays 50–75% on digital products like courses and software. That difference is huge — a $200 course at 70% commission earns you $140. A $200 laptop at 4% earns you $8.

The smartest beginners promote both networks. Amazon converts more reliably (everyone trusts Amazon), Digistore24 pays much higher commissions when sales happen.

How Do You Get People to Click Your Links?

This is where content comes in. The most reliable way to get clicks is to write helpful content — product reviews, buying guides, comparison articles, and how-to guides. People search Google for "best laptop under $1000" or "is the AZ Code worth it" and land on your article. They read it, trust your recommendation, and click your link.

You can also drive traffic through Pinterest, YouTube, TikTok, and Reddit. Each platform has millions of people looking for recommendations every single day. Your job is to be the person they find.

How Much Can You Realistically Earn?

Let's be honest about numbers. Months 1–3: Usually $0–$50 while you build content and Google starts indexing you. Months 4–6: $100–$500/month if you've published 20+ quality articles. Months 6–12: $500–$2,000/month becomes realistic with consistent effort. Year 2+: $2,000–$10,000+/month is achievable for those who stick with it.

Income depends on your niche, traffic sources, and how aggressively you publish. The people earning $10K+/month are almost always the ones who treated it like a real business from day one.

Final Verdict — Is Affiliate Marketing Worth It?

Absolutely — but only if you treat it like a real business. The people who fail at affiliate marketing expect overnight results. The people who succeed publish consistently, choose products they genuinely believe in, and give it 6 months to build momentum.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much money can I make with affiliate marketing?

Realistic income for a consistent beginner is $0 in months 1-3, $100-500 in months 4-6, and $500-$2,000+ per month by month 12. Top affiliates earn six and seven figures, but consistency matters more than talent.

Do I need a website to start affiliate marketing?

Not technically — you can use Pinterest, YouTube, TikTok, or Reddit. But a simple website (free on GitHub Pages or WordPress) gives you long-term SEO traffic that compounds. Most successful affiliates use a website plus one social platform.

Is affiliate marketing legal?

Yes, fully legal in the US, UK, EU, and most countries. The FTC requires you to disclose affiliate relationships clearly — usually with a simple disclosure statement at the top of any article containing affiliate links.

How long does it take to make money?

Most beginners see their first commission within 30-60 days if they publish consistently. Stable monthly income typically starts around month 4-6. The "overnight success" stories are almost always either lies or people with existing audiences.

What's better — Amazon Associates or Digistore24?

Both — combine them. Amazon converts more reliably but pays 1-8% commission. Digistore24 pays 50-75% on digital products but converts at lower rates. The smartest beginners promote both.
