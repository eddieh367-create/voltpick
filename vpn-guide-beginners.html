What is a VPN and Do You Actually Need One?

A VPN — Virtual Private Network — is a tool that hides your internet activity from anyone trying to spy on it. That includes your internet provider, hackers on public WiFi, advertisers tracking your every move, and in some countries, government surveillance. In 2026 with data breaches happening constantly, a VPN is no longer optional — it is essential.

If you use public WiFi at cafes, airports, or hotels — you need a VPN. If you work from home and handle sensitive data — you need a VPN. If you value your privacy online at all — you need a VPN. The good news is they are cheap, easy to set up, and make a significant difference to your online security.

"Using public WiFi without a VPN is like having a conversation in a crowded room and expecting nobody to listen."

How Does a VPN Actually Work?

When you connect to the internet normally your data travels from your device through your internet provider to whatever website you are visiting. Every step of that journey can be monitored. A VPN creates an encrypted tunnel between your device and a secure server. Your data travels through that tunnel — scrambled and unreadable to anyone trying to intercept it.

From the outside it looks like your internet traffic is coming from the VPN server not your device. This hides your real location and identity. It also means websites cannot track your real IP address — one of the most common ways advertisers build profiles on you.

VPN Router vs VPN App — Which Should You Use?

Most people start with a VPN app on their phone or laptop — services like NordVPN or ExpressVPN. These are great for personal use and cost around $3–$5 per month. But they only protect the device the app is installed on.

A VPN router protects every device in your home automatically — your phone, laptop, smart TV, gaming console, and smart home devices all get protected the moment they connect to your WiFi. For a complete home security setup a VPN router is the best long term investment. You can find excellent ones on Amazon for under $100.
