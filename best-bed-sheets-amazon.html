What to Look for When Buying Bed Sheets

Thread Count — More Is Not Always Better

The myth that higher thread count always means better sheets is widespread but inaccurate. Thread count between 200 and 400 is the sweet spot for most people — high enough for a smooth feel without being so dense the fabric traps heat. Some manufacturers inflate thread counts using multi-ply threads which does not improve quality.

Material

Microfiber — soft, affordable, wrinkle resistant, durable. Best for most budgets. Our top pick uses a high quality microfiber blend. Cotton — breathable, natural, gets softer with washing. Egyptian cotton and Pima cotton are the premium options. Bamboo — naturally cooling, good for hot sleepers, more expensive.

Certifications

Look for Oeko-Tex Standard 100 certification — this ensures the sheets have been tested and verified free from harmful chemicals. Particularly important for people with sensitive skin or allergies.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should you wash bed sheets?
Most sleep experts recommend washing bed sheets once per week. At minimum every two weeks. Regular washing removes dead skin cells, dust mites and oils that accumulate over time and can affect skin health and sleep quality.
What is the best bed sheet material for hot sleepers?
For hot sleepers look for lightweight breathable materials — cotton percale, bamboo or high quality microfiber with a cooling weave. Avoid sateen weaves and high thread count sheets as these tend to trap heat. Our top pick has a specifically cooling breathable weave suitable for warm sleepers.
Do more expensive bed sheets last longer?
Not necessarily. Price is not always a reliable indicator of durability. Our top pick performs as well as sheets costing two to three times more in our wash durability tests. The key factors are material quality and weave construction — not price.
