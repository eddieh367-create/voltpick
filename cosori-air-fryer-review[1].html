What Is the Cosori TurboBlaze?

The Cosori TurboBlaze is a 9-in-1 air fryer with a 6 quart capacity, smart shake reminders, and precise temperature control from 90°F to 450°F. It retails for around $80 to $100 on Amazon making it one of the most feature-packed air fryers available at this price point.

The headline feature is the TurboBlaze fan technology — a high speed fan system that circulates hot air faster and more evenly than standard air fryers. In our testing this translated to noticeably crispier results with shorter cooking times compared to the other models we tested.

Cooking Performance — The Most Important Test

We cooked 12 different foods across two weeks — frozen fries, fresh chicken wings, salmon fillets, broccoli, garlic bread, homemade cookies, beef jerky, roasted vegetables, frozen pizza rolls, chicken breast, pork chops and bacon. The Cosori produced excellent results across almost everything we tested.

Frozen fries came out genuinely crispy on the outside and fluffy inside — better than oven results in half the time. Chicken wings were the standout — the skin was properly crispy without drying out the meat, which is difficult to achieve consistently. Vegetables roasted beautifully with caramelization that you normally only get in a very hot oven.

The only area where results were slightly inconsistent was very thin items like bacon — the uneven shape meant some parts cooked faster than others. This is a common limitation of basket style air fryers rather than a specific Cosori problem.

The 9 Cooking Functions

The Cosori TurboBlaze covers: Air Fry, Roast, Bake, Broil, Dehydrate, Frozen foods, Proof, Reheat and Keep Warm. In practice the most useful are air fry, roast, bake and reheat. The dehydrate function is genuinely useful for making jerky or dried fruit. The proof function for bread dough is an unexpected but welcome addition.

Capacity — Is 6QT Enough?

The 6QT basket comfortably fits a whole small chicken, two large salmon fillets side by side, or a full batch of wings for 4 people. For most families of 3 to 5 people this is the right size — large enough for family meals without taking up excessive counter space. If you regularly cook for 6 or more people you would want to look at an 8QT model.

Ease of Use and Controls

The digital touchscreen is responsive and clear. Presets for common foods make it simple to use without consulting the manual — just select your food type, adjust time and temperature if needed, and press start. The shake reminder is a standout feature — the fryer beeps and pauses to remind you to shake the basket halfway through cooking, ensuring even results without you having to remember.

Cleaning

The basket and crisper plate are both dishwasher safe which makes cleanup genuinely easy. Hand washing takes about 2 minutes. The exterior wipes clean easily. There is no non-stick coating to worry about — the basket uses a food grade coating that held up well across two weeks of daily use.

Who Should Buy the Cosori TurboBlaze?

Buy it if: You cook for 3 to 5 people regularly, you want to replace your oven for everyday cooking, you want the crispiest results possible under $100, or you want versatility across multiple cooking methods.

Skip it if: You only cook for 1 to 2 people and counter space is tight — the Ninja AF101 4QT is a better fit. Or if you are on a very tight budget — the Instant Vortex 4QT delivers solid results for under $60.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does the Cosori air fryer take to cook chicken wings?
Fresh chicken wings take approximately 22 to 25 minutes at 400°F in the Cosori TurboBlaze — significantly faster than a conventional oven and with crispier skin. Shake halfway through for even results.
Does the Cosori air fryer require preheating?
The Cosori heats up very quickly and most foods do not require preheating. For best results with items that need a very hot surface — like steaks — a 3 minute preheat at maximum temperature is recommended.
Is the Cosori TurboBlaze better than the Cosori Pro?
Yes — the TurboBlaze is the newer model with upgraded fan technology that produces faster and more even cooking results than the older Pro model. If you are choosing between the two the TurboBlaze is worth the small price difference.
