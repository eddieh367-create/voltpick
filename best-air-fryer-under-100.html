What to Look For When Buying an Air Fryer Under $100

1. Capacity — This Matters More Than You Think

The most common mistake people make when buying an air fryer is choosing one that is too small. As a general rule:

  • 1 to 2 people — 2QT to 3QT is sufficient
  • 3 to 4 people — 4QT to 5QT is the sweet spot
  • 5 or more people — 6QT or larger is recommended
  • Batch cooking for meal prep — always go larger than you think you need

2. Temperature Range

A wider temperature range gives you more cooking flexibility. The Cosori goes from 90°F to 450°F — the low end is useful for proofing bread dough and dehydrating, while the high end gives you a proper sear on meats. Cheaper models often only go from 200°F to 400°F which limits what you can cook.

3. Number of Cooking Functions

More functions means more versatility. A 9-in-1 air fryer like the Cosori replaces multiple appliances — air fryer, oven, dehydrator, roaster. If counter space is limited this matters a lot. If you just want to air fry your frozen food a simpler 3 or 4 function model is perfectly fine.

4. Ease of Cleaning

Look for a model with a dishwasher safe basket. Air fryer baskets get greasy and need regular cleaning — if it is not dishwasher safe you will dread cleaning it and use it less. All our recommended models have dishwasher safe components.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the Cosori air fryer worth the money?
Yes — for most households the Cosori TurboBlaze offers the best combination of performance, capacity and features under $100. The cooking results are noticeably better than cheaper models and the 9 functions make it genuinely versatile.
What size air fryer do I need for a family of 4?
For a family of 4 we recommend a minimum of 5QT — preferably 6QT. The Cosori TurboBlaze at 6QT is our top recommendation for families as it can cook a full batch of chicken wings or a whole small chicken in one go.
How much electricity does an air fryer use?
Most air fryers use between 1,200 and 1,700 watts — significantly less than a conventional oven which uses 2,000 to 5,000 watts. Switching from an oven to an air fryer for everyday cooking can noticeably reduce your electricity bill over time.
Can you cook frozen food in an air fryer?
Yes — and this is one of the best use cases for an air fryer. Frozen fries, chicken nuggets, fish fillets and vegetables all cook faster and crispier in an air fryer than in a conventional oven, without needing any oil.
Is air frying actually healthier than frying?
Yes — significantly. Air frying uses up to 75% less oil than traditional deep frying while achieving similar crispiness. This reduces the calorie and fat content of fried foods considerably without sacrificing taste or texture.
