What to Look for When Buying a Home Router

WiFi 6 vs WiFi 5

WiFi 6 is the current standard and handles more simultaneous connections faster and more efficiently than WiFi 5. If you have more than 10 connected devices — phones, laptops, smart TVs, smart home devices — WiFi 6 makes a noticeable difference. All our recommended routers support WiFi 6.

Coverage Area

Match the router coverage to your home size. A single router covers most apartments and small homes up to 2,500 square feet. For larger homes or multi-story properties a mesh system with multiple nodes eliminates dead zones more effectively than a single powerful router.

Number of Devices

Count every connected device in your home — phones, tablets, laptops, smart TVs, streaming devices, smart speakers, security cameras, thermostats. The average home in 2026 has 25 to 35 connected devices. Choose a router rated for significantly more than your current count to allow for growth.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should you replace your home router?
Every 3 to 5 years is a good rule of thumb. Router technology advances significantly in this timeframe — a 5-year-old router running WiFi 5 on outdated firmware handles significantly fewer devices less efficiently than a modern WiFi 6 router. If your internet feels slow despite a fast plan your router is often the bottleneck.
Will a better router make my internet faster?
A better router will not increase the speed your internet provider delivers to your home — that is determined by your plan. However it will more efficiently distribute that speed across all your devices and eliminate bottlenecks that cause slow speeds on individual devices even when your connection is fast.
Is mesh WiFi worth it?
For homes larger than 2,500 square feet or multi-story homes with dead zones — yes. Mesh systems eliminate dead zones more reliably than a single powerful router. For smaller apartments and homes a single good router like the TP-Link AX55 is more than sufficient and significantly cheaper.
